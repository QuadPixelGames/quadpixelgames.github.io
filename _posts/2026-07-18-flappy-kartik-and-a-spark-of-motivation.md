So I was just randomly going through old files today — you know, the kind of aimless digging you do when you're procrastinating on something else — and I found it.

**Flappy Kartik.**

I honestly forgot this thing existed. It was this dumb little Flappy Bird clone we made where Kartik is the bird. Super janky. Kartik hates it. Probably broken in half the browsers out there. But I opened it anyway, and I sat there playing it for way longer than I'm going to say.

And something about it just... hit different.

Like, this was *us*. Four guys who thought it'd be funny to make a game where one of our friends flaps through pipes. No grand ambitions, no roadmap, no investors. Just a weird idea that made us laugh, slapped together with digital duct tape (I guess) and put on the here on the internet.

I don't really know when QuadPixel went quiet. It just.. did? Exams came, projects stopped, we stopped talking about it, and suddenly files modified over a year ago.

But finding Flappy Kartik reminded why we started this at all. Not to ship polished products or even go viral. Just to make dumb, chaotic, somewhat fun stuff and put it out there. And learn from it.


So yeah. No promises. I'm not saying we're gonna make hundreds of projects. I'm just saying.. I wanna start making stuff again. Small stuff. Super weird stuff. QuadPixel stuff.

Let's see where this goes.

— Aatiksh
